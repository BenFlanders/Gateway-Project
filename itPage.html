IT Opportunities In Australia

Australia often gets a bad reputation when it comes to internet and technology (even the way our toilets flush gets made fun of). But IT opportunities are currently blowing up in Australia, and major advancements are being made to technology! Listed down below are some factors of the recent expansion, as well as how some of Australia's major cities are leaving their mark in the world of IT!

OPPORTUNITIES RISING?

Over the past 18 months, around 250,000 job opportunities were created in Australia. The Department of Employment of Australia has forecasted growth of 12.8 percent in the demand of IT professionals. As per the predictions, the highest growth nationwide is expected to be witnessed for computer network professionals.

TECHNOLOGICAL INFLUENCE

Technology is an influential, multi-faceted industry focusing on meeting consumer demand. With the rise in demand and stature of software developers and programmers, jobs in the IT industry are expected to be among the hot jobs in the economy.

MELBOURNE

In Australia, Melbourne is considered to be the center of technology, education, research and logistics. Being the second largest city of Australia, Melbourne is home to several global companies in the IT sector. It has also been named as the best city in Australia for international students and workforce.

SYDNEY

Sydney is also a destination for professionals looking for employment in IT. Sydney tops the table in terms of IT salaries. Services of IT companies are being heavily used for domestic and global growth of companies in health technology, financial services, e-learning and more.

IT Facts About Australia

  • As of January 2019, there were 21.74 million Internet users in Australia, constituting 87% of the Australian population. 93% of these internet users use the internet every day
  • Internet users in Australia spend an average of 5.5 hours per day using internet services, including approximately 3 hours viewing streaming services and internet broadcasts, ~45 minutes streaming music, and ~1.5 hours using social media
  • Download volumes from mobile devices are growing particularly quickly. There was 40% growth in data downloaded from mobile devices from June 2017 to June 2018
  • Social media is now an integral part of Australian society, with 18 million active users of social media websites (69% of the population) as of January 2019. Facebook is the most popular social media platform, with ~16 million monthly users of the website.
  • A 2018 mobile consumer survey found that ~90% of Australians have direct access to a smartphone. This was more than 3 times the rate of smartphone access in 2008.
  • Google is another major factor in how Australians experience the Internet. Google is by far the most used search engine in Australia, holding a market share of 95% as of May 2019, followed by Bing with 3.5% and DuckDuckGo with 0.52%
